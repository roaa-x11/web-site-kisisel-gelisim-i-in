Spend Time Outside

today due to the intense work tempo and digitalization, we spend much more time indoors than before. However, the positive contribution of living close to green areas to mental health has been proven by many studies. In fact, one study proved that just looking at pictures of nature activates the parts of the brain related to happiness, positive thinking and emotional balance. In addition, spending time outside of the hours of 11:00-16:00, when the damage from ultraviolet rays is at a minimum level, and thus benefiting from sunlight, will meet our vitamin D needs and reduce the risk of depression.

Put Your Feelings on Paper

Negative emotions can sometimes be so strong that they can take all your energy for months or even years. A scientific study conducted in Spain observed whether turning emotions into material would cause a change in mood. For this purpose, the emotional changes experienced by subjects when they wrote their negative thoughts on a piece of paper and threw it in the trash were observed. The results were surprising. A relief was observed in the psychological states of those who put their negative feelings on paper and threw it in the trash. It turned out that those who wrote their positive feelings on paper felt better.
